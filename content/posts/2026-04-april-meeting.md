## Demonstration: Ribbon Finials

![Peggy Schmid with a finished ribbon finial hollow form](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-1.jpeg "Peggy Schmid showing a completed ribbon finial piece")

Peggy Schmid from Atlanta demonstrated making ribbon finials. Her featured tool was a Sorby thread guide capable of cutting 10, 16, and 22 TPI male and female threads in wood.

![The Sorby thread guide](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-4.jpeg "Sorby thread guide — cuts 10, 16, and 22 TPI threads")

### Making the Insert

1. Turn an "insert" about 1-1/4" diameter. Mark center and hollow the ID. The insert serves as a transition piece between the finial and a hollow form. Bradford pear, dogwood, ebony, and cocobolo are recommended insert materials.

2. Part the insert from the block.

![Turning the insert on the lathe](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-3.jpeg "Shaping the insert between centers")

3. Remount the insert and clean-cut the face. Size the bore for threading using the Sorby thread guide.

4. Chase threads with the thread die — hold the die slightly below center and cut until a clear peak is visible. Set the lathe to very slow speed for thread cutting. Set the insert aside.

![Two inserts showing the hollowed bore and threaded interior](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-5.jpeg "Completed inserts — hollowed and threaded")

![Threading at the lathe](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-6.jpeg "Chasing threads with the Sorby die")

![Checking thread fit in the chuck](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-7.jpeg "Testing the thread fit")

### Threading the Finial

5. Mount finial stock on the lathe. Using the thread guide, size a tenon to match the insert thread. Cut a thread relief groove at the base of the tenon and add a slight bevel on the end. Cut male threads onto the tenon at minimum lathe speed with the die slightly below center. Check fit with the insert. Once acceptable, wrap threads with painters tape.

   *Tool prep: Before threading, grind away the half tooth from the thread die.*

![Finial tenon with thread relief groove and taped threads](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-8.jpeg "Sized tenon with thread relief and painters tape wrap")

![Checking the threaded finial fit with the insert](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-10.jpeg "Test-fitting the threaded finial to the insert")

6. Mount a waste block and cut a mortise for a jam fit with the taped threads. The tape acts as a thread release when the finial is complete. Insert the finial.

### Hollowing and Shaping

7. Core and drill the finial with a machine drill bit — do not use a brad point bit. Apply wax to the drill bit as needed for a smooth cut.

![Drilling the finial with a machine bit](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-9.jpeg "Core drilling the finial — wax the bit for a smooth cut")

8. Size the finial wall thickness by turning the OD, using something similar to a live cone center in the tailstock. Leave a pocket at the bottom of the finial — this holds a dowel rod during ribbon carving.

![Turning the finial OD to final wall thickness](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-11.jpeg "Shaping the finial wall with a gouge")

9. Sand and polish the finial after shaping. Add any artwork you prefer.

### Carving the Ribbon

10. Apply painters tape in a candy cane / barber pole stripe along the finial. This marks the ribbon swirl layout.

![Taped finial with barber pole stripe layout](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-12.jpeg "Painters tape marking the ribbon swirl pattern")

11. Remove the finial and insert a dowel rod sized to match the ID. The dowel supports the finial during carving.

12. Using a power carver, create a pathway along the taped layout — a gentle touch is essential. Cutting wheels can be sourced from Harbor Freight. Leave a portion of the dowel pocket untouched so waste ribbon material releases cleanly, avoiding the need to carve a relief into the transition from swirl to finial base.

![Power carving the ribbon pathway](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-13.jpeg "Carving the ribbon with a rotary tool and cutting wheel")

13. Gently remove tape and waste once loosened from the finial. The ribbon is fragile. Sand as needed.

![Removing waste ribbon material with carving tools](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-14.jpeg "Delicate work — freeing the carved ribbon from the finial")

![Finished hollow forms with ribbon finials](/images/posts/2026-04-18/image-15.jpeg "Completed ribbon finial hollow forms with the power carver")
